AMC SERVICE AGREEMENT

Appraisaldesk.com LLC d/b/a Appraisal Desk

Effective Date: Upon Electronic Acceptance

Parties:

  • Company: Appraisaldesk.com LLC d/b/a Appraisal Desk, a Utah limited liability company with its principal place of business at 13894 South Bangerter Parkway, STE 200, Draper, UT 84020 ("Company," "we," "us," or "our")
  • AMC: Recorded as the entity signing up for Appraisal Desk platform and electronically accepting this service agreement.

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company operates a software platform that facilitates appraisal orders between lenders, appraisal management companies, and appraisers; and

WHEREAS, AMC is licensed and qualified to provide appraisal management services; and

WHEREAS, AMC desires to receive and manage appraisal orders through the Platform as a subcontractor to Company; and

WHEREAS, the parties wish to establish the terms and conditions governing this subcontractor relationship;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS

"AMC" means an appraisal management company licensed or registered as required by applicable law.

"Appraiser" means a licensed or certified real estate appraiser.

"Lender" means a mortgage lender or loan originator.

"Order" means a request for appraisal services submitted through the Platform.

"Platform" means Company's proprietary software platform and related services.

"USPAP" means the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice.

2. SUBCONTRACTOR RELATIONSHIP

2.1 Nature of Relationship

AMC agrees to work as an independent subcontractor to Company for the fulfillment of appraisal orders received through the Platform. This relationship is not an employment relationship, partnership, or joint venture.

2.2 Independent Contractor Status

AMC acknowledges that:

  • Company will issue Form 1099 for payments made to AMC
  • AMC is responsible for all applicable taxes on payments received
  • AMC maintains its own business license, insurance, and regulatory compliance
  • AMC controls the methods and means of performing appraisal management services

2.3 Subcontractor Authority

As Company's subcontractor, AMC has authority to:

  • Accept orders assigned through the Platform
  • Engage qualified appraisers to complete orders
  • Manage the appraisal process from order to delivery
  • Communicate with lenders regarding order status and requirements

3. ORDER MANAGEMENT RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 Order Acceptance

AMC agrees to:

  • Accept orders assigned through the Platform within specified timeframes
  • Acknowledge receipt of orders through the Platform
  • Provide estimated completion dates for accepted orders
  • Notify Company immediately if unable to complete assigned orders

3.2 Appraiser Assignment

AMC shall:

  • Assign orders only to qualified, licensed appraisers
  • Verify appraiser licensing and geographic competency
  • Ensure appraiser compliance with applicable regulations
  • Maintain documentation of appraiser qualifications

3.3 Quality Control and Management

AMC is responsible for:

  • Reviewing all completed appraisals for compliance with USPAP and applicable standards
  • Ensuring timely completion of orders within agreed timeframes
  • Managing communication between lenders and appraisers
  • Coordinating any required revisions or corrections
  • Maintaining detailed records of all order activities

3.4 Order Delivery

AMC shall:

  • Submit completed appraisals through the Platform
  • Ensure all required documents and certifications are included
  • Provide final invoice and completion documentation
  • Respond to post-delivery questions or issues

4. BILLING RESPONSIBILITY AND PAYMENT OPTIONS

4.1 Payment Option Selection

For each lender relationship, AMC must choose one of the following billing options:

Option 1: Platform Bills Lender Full Amount

  • Requires valid Lender Billing Authorization
  • Company collects full order amount from lender
  • Company distributes AMC portion after order completion
  • AMC receives payment minus Company's platform fee

Option 2: Platform Bills Lender for Platform Fee Only

  • Requires valid Lender Billing Authorization
  • Company bills lender only for platform fees
  • AMC handles separate billing to lender for appraisal services
  • AMC pays Company platform fee separately

Option 3: AMC Pays Platform Fee Directly

  • No lender authorization required
  • AMC billed directly for platform fees
  • AMC handles all billing to lender independently
  • Payment due upon order completion

4.2 Lender Authorization Requirements

For Options 1 and 2, AMC must:

  • Obtain valid Lender Billing Authorization before order placement
  • Provide Company with executed authorization documents
  • Maintain current and valid authorizations for all lender relationships
  • Update authorizations as required

4.3 Billing Authorization Responsibility

CRITICAL REQUIREMENT: AMC is solely responsible for obtaining lender billing authorizations. If AMC cannot obtain required authorization from a lender, AMC must use Option 3 (direct payment) for that lender relationship.

4.4 Default Payment Option

If no billing authorization is provided for a lender relationship, Option 3 (AMC pays directly) will be applied automatically.

5. PAYMENT DISTRIBUTION AND TERMS

5.1 Billing Frequency and Pricing

Billing Schedule: Platform fees are billed daily or weekly as determined by Company based on order volume and account activity.

Per-Order Pricing: All platform fees are charged per individual order. Specific pricing will be discussed individually with AMC and uploaded to the Platform for AMC's review and acceptance before any orders are processed.

Integration Fees: No setup fees apply for standard integrations currently supported by the Platform. If AMC requires a custom integration not currently supported, a one-time integration fee of $500 will apply to cover development and implementation costs.

5.2 Payment Processing

Company will:

  • Process payments according to the selected billing option
  • Distribute funds to AMC within 5 business days of order completion and payment receipt
  • Provide detailed payment statements and documentation
  • Handle all payment processing, including credit card and ACH transactions

5.3 Payment Failure Responsibility and Late Fees

AMC is responsible for all costs and fees when AMC is the paying party, including:

  • Failed ACH transaction fees ($10 per failed attempt)
  • Credit card chargeback fees
  • Payment dispute costs
  • Collection fees and legal costs

Late Payment Interest: Any amounts not paid by AMC when due shall accrue interest at the rate of ten percent (10%) per annum from the due date until paid in full.

5.4 Tax Reporting

Company will issue Form 1099-NEC to AMC for all payments made during the calendar year, as required by applicable tax laws.

6. NETWORK OVERFLOW OBLIGATIONS

6.1 Overflow Acceptance

AMC agrees to accept overflow orders when:

  • Other AMCs in the network cannot fulfill orders due to capacity constraints
  • Geographic coverage gaps exist in the network
  • Urgent orders require additional capacity
  • Company determines additional resources are needed

6.2 Capacity Maintenance

AMC shall:

  • Maintain adequate appraiser network to handle assigned orders plus reasonable overflow
  • Provide Company with current capacity information
  • Update geographic coverage areas as they change
  • Notify Company of capacity constraints in advance when possible

6.3 Geographic Coverage

AMC shall specify and maintain:

  • Primary geographic coverage areas
  • Secondary coverage areas for overflow orders
  • Property types and specializations available
  • Capacity limitations by geographic area

6.4 Volume Flexibility

AMC acknowledges that order volume may fluctuate significantly and agrees to:

  • Scale operations to accommodate volume changes
  • Maintain flexibility in appraiser assignments
  • Communicate capacity issues promptly
  • Work with Company to resolve capacity constraints

7. APPRAISER MANAGEMENT REQUIREMENTS

7.1 Appraiser Vetting

AMC shall verify and maintain documentation for all appraisers, including:

  • Current license or certification status
  • Geographic competency and coverage areas
  • Experience and specialization qualifications
  • Regulatory compliance history
  • Performance and quality metrics

7.2 Insurance Requirements

AMC must ensure all appraisers carry:

  • Errors and omissions insurance equal to the value of properties being appraised
  • Current and valid insurance coverage
  • Proper insurance documentation on file
  • Immediate notification of coverage lapses or changes

7.3 Performance Monitoring

AMC shall:

  • Monitor appraiser performance and quality
  • Implement improvement plans for underperforming appraisers
  • Remove non-compliant appraisers from the network
  • Maintain performance records and documentation

7.4 Regulatory Compliance

AMC ensures all appraisers:

  • Comply with applicable state and federal regulations
  • Maintain current licensing and certification
  • Follow USPAP standards and requirements
  • Participate in required continuing education

8. DISPUTE RESOLUTION RESPONSIBILITIES

8.1 Primary Responsibility

AMC has primary responsibility for handling ALL disputes between lenders and appraisers, including:

  • Quality concerns and revision requests
  • Timeline and delivery issues
  • Valuation questions and challenges
  • Technical and procedural problems

8.2 Dispute Management Process

AMC shall:

  • Respond to disputes within 24 hours of notification
  • Investigate issues thoroughly and objectively
  • Communicate with all parties to reach resolution
  • Document dispute resolution actions and outcomes
  • Escalate unresolvable issues to Company only as a last resort

8.3 Platform Non-Involvement

Company does not get involved in quality, performance, or valuation disputes. AMC acknowledges that Company's role is limited to platform services and payment processing.

8.4 Resolution Authority

AMC has authority to:

  • Order revisions or corrections from appraisers
  • Negotiate resolution terms with lenders
  • Authorize partial refunds or credits when appropriate
  • Replace appraisers on orders when necessary

9. LENDER RELATIONSHIP MANAGEMENT

9.1 Authorization Acquisition

AMC is responsible for:

  • Obtaining required Lender Billing Authorizations
  • Maintaining current and valid authorizations
  • Providing Company with executed authorization documents
  • Updating Company when authorizations expire or change

9.2 Relationship Maintenance

AMC shall:

  • Maintain existing business relationships with lenders
  • Serve as primary point of contact for lender communications
  • Coordinate with lender systems and processes
  • Provide customer service and support to lenders

9.3 Integration Support

AMC may assist with:

  • Lender onboarding and training
  • System integration and setup
  • Process development and optimization
  • Ongoing relationship management

10. PLATFORM AVAILABILITY AND SUPPORT

10.1 Service Level Target

Company targets 99.9% platform uptime during business hours (8:00 AM to 6:00 PM Mountain Time, Monday through Friday), excluding planned maintenance windows. Company will provide reasonable advance notice of planned maintenance when possible.

10.2 Support Services

Company provides:

  • Technical support for platform issues

Company does not commit to specific customer support hours but will use reasonable efforts to respond to critical issues promptly.

AMC shall protect all confidential information, including:
